## Growth Drivers

### 1. Regulatory Breakthroughs
- **2024**: US legalized election betting
- **May 2025**: CFTC dropped Kalshi appeal (election contracts permitted)
- **2025**: Polymarket acquired DCM license for US re-entry ($112M acquisition)

### 2. Institutional Adoption
- **ICE (NYSE parent)**: $2B investment in Polymarket
- **Coinbase**: Launching prediction markets for US users
- **DraftKings**: Acquiring Railbird, partnering with Polymarket
- **Gemini**: Planning prediction market contracts

### 3. Platform Partnerships
- **Polymarket**: Official prediction partner of X (Twitter)
- **Kalshi**: Robinhood integration, xAI/Grok partnership
- **Kalshi**: Coinbase custody for USDC

### 4. Market Expansion
- Sports markets now 90% of Kalshi volume
- Post-election shift from politics to sports/entertainment
- AI integration driving new use cases

## Challenges & Risks

### Liquidity Concerns
- Markets still tiny compared to multi-trillion-dollar stock/crypto markets
- Thin liquidity can spur volatility
- Retail vulnerable to manipulation by whales

### Regulatory Uncertainty
- State vs federal jurisdiction battles
- New York AB 9251 restricting political contracts
- SEC vs CFTC jurisdiction questions

### Integrity Issues
- Columbia study: 25% of Polymarket volume may be wash trading
- Oracle manipulation incidents ($7M Polymarket loss)
- UMA voting power concentration
