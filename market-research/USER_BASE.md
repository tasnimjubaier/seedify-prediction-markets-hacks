# Prediction Market User Base & Demographics

## Executive Summary

Prediction markets have grown from 29K monthly active users (June 2024) to **477K+ monthly active traders (October 2025)**. The user base is dominated by crypto-native retail traders, with a shift toward sports markets and increasing "power user" concentration.

## User Demographics

### Geographic Distribution

**Pre-2025 (Polymarket)**:
- Non-US users only (since 2022 CFTC settlement)
- Global distribution

**2025 Shift**:
- Polymarket re-entering US market
- Kalshi operating in all 50 states
- International expansion planned

### User Profile

#### Primary User Base
- **Crypto-native traders**: Already familiar with wallets, USDC
- **Retail speculators**: Individual traders, not institutions
- **Younger demographics**: Especially active in social media/trend betting
- **Political junkies**: Heavy engagement during elections

#### Emerging Segments
- **Sports bettors**: Shifting from traditional sportsbooks
- **Power users**: Smaller group, higher volume per account
- **Institutional hedgers**: Emerging use case

### Behavioral Characteristics

Based on crypto trader research:

1. **Speculative nature**: Returns driven by retail speculation
2. **Social media influence**: Strong correlation with Twitter/X, Reddit sentiment
3. **Momentum trading**: Behavioral patterns follow market trends
4. **Information asymmetry**: "Amateur" investors interpret information differently

## Wash Trading & Bot Activity

### Columbia University Study Findings (Nov 2025)

| Metric | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Suspected wash trading | ~25% of total volume |
| Peak fake volume | 60% of weekly volume (Dec 2024) |
| Flagged wallets | 14% of 1.26M total |
| Most affected | Sports & election markets (>90% in some weeks) |

### Why Polymarket is Vulnerable
1. No identity verification (KYC)
2. No trading fees
3. USDC-only (easy to automate)
4. Airdrop speculation incentivizes volume

### Implications
- Actual user base may be smaller than reported
- Volume metrics potentially inflated
- "Wisdom of crowd" accuracy may be overstated

## User Needs & Pain Points

### Current Frustrations

1. **Slow resolution**: 24-48h for oracle settlement
2. **Manipulation concerns**: Oracle attacks, whale dominance
3. **UX complexity**: Wallets, gas fees, bridging
4. **Limited markets**: Only well-defined, verifiable events
5. **Liquidity issues**: Wide spreads, slippage on large orders

### Desired Features

1. **Faster resolution**: Minutes, not hours/days
2. **Better UX**: Gasless, mobile-first, no wallet setup
3. **More markets**: Subjective events, niche topics
4. **Lower fees**: Currently 0-2% platform fees
5. **Social features**: Share predictions, leaderboards

## User Segments for Targeting

### High-Value Segments

#### 1. Crypto Power Users
- **Size**: ~50,000 (top 10%)
- **Behavior**: $5,000+ avg trades
- **Needs**: Low fees, deep liquidity, fast execution
- **Acquisition**: DEX/DeFi communities, CT (Crypto Twitter)

#### 2. Sports Bettors (Crossover)
- **Size**: Millions (from traditional sportsbooks)
- **Behavior**: Frequent small bets
- **Needs**: Familiar UX, mobile app, fiat on-ramp
- **Acquisition**: Sports media, betting forums

#### 3. GenZ/Social Traders
- **Size**: Growing rapidly
- **Behavior**: Trend-driven, social sharing
- **Needs**: TikTok-style UX, viral mechanics
- **Acquisition**: Social media influencers, memes

#### 4. Institutional/Hedgers
- **Size**: Emerging
- **Behavior**: Large positions for hedging
- **Needs**: Compliance, deep liquidity, API access
- **Acquisition**: Direct sales, conferences
